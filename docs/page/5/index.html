1. Limit of Sequences Given a function $f:\mathbb{N}_{+}\to \mathbb{R}$, we call $f( \mathbb{N}_{+})$ as a sequence, enumerated as $a_1,a_2,\ldots$, denoted by $\{a_n\}$. Definition 1 (Convergence) We say a sequence $\\{a_n\\}$ is convergent to $a$ if for any $\epsilon>0$, there exits a constant $N$ s.t. for any $n\geq N$, $$ |a_n - a|<\epsilon. $$ And the point $a$ is called the limit of the sequence $\{a_n\}$, denoted by $\lim_{n\to\infty}a_n = a$. Mathematical Analysis | 1 Real Numbers and Functions

1. Real Numbers Definition 1 (Bounds) $S\in\mathbb{R}$. We say that $S$ is bounded above if there exits $M$ s.t. for every $x\in S$ $x\leq M$, and $M$ is called the upper bond of $S$. Similarly, we say that $S$ is bounded from below if $x\geq M$, and $M$ is called the lower bound of $S$. We say that $S$ is bounded if its upper bounds and lower bounds exist. Definition 2 (Supremum and infimum) Let $\eta$ be an upper bound of $S$. If for any $\alpha<\eta$ there exists $x_0\in S$ s.t. $x_0>\alpha$, then $\eta$ is called the supremum of $S$, denoted by $\eta = \sup S$. Similarly, $\eta$ is the infimum of $S$, denoted by $\eta = \inf S$, if for any $\alpha>\eta$, there exits $x_0\in S$ s.t. $x_0<\alpha$. Theorem 3 (Existence) If $S$ has an upper bound, then $\sup S$ exists. If $S$ has an lower bound, then $\inf S$ exists. Corollary 4 Given nonempty sets $A,B$. If $\forall x\in A,\forall y\in B$ $x\leq y$, then $\sup A$ and $\inf B$ exist. Moreover, $\sup A\leq \inf B$. proof: The existence of $\sup A$ and $\inf B$ is immediate by Theorem 3.
